AI Course — Group Knowledge Exchange

Flavour Groups

Connecting students with shared AI experiment interests to learn from each other's findings
Each student has been exploring their own AI experiment direction this semester. This initiative groups students with similar topics together so they can share outcomes, learn from each other's findings, and collectively build deeper understanding — with teachers joining as participants too.

How It Works — 5 Steps

1
Submit Your Pitch
Write a short summary (roughly one A4 page) of your experiment, approach, and findings — your "flavour". Submit it to the Canvas assignment "Pitch of your personal flavour". AI can help you generate this summary. Make sure to include relevant keywords so your pitch can be matched with others.
Deliverable: 1-page pitch on Canvas
2
AI-Assisted Grouping
All pitches are analysed by AI to identify shared themes and topics. Students are grouped based on similarity, with a maximum of 6 students per group to keep sessions small and productive.
Groups formed by teachers based on AI analysis
3
Vote on Flavours You Want to See
A form is published on Teams (similar to the project selection form at the start of the semester). Browse all students' experiment pitches and vote for the ones you'd most like to hear results from. Teachers participate in this vote too — they can join any flavour group that interests them.
Voting form on Teams — link published separately
4
Matched Groups Schedule a Sharing Session
Once groups are matched, each group organises its own sharing session. The goal is to exchange knowledge and let others learn from your findings. The format is flexible — groups can decide what works best for them, and multiple shorter sessions are encouraged over a single long one.
Suggested format: 5 min pitch per person → open discussion
5
Document for Your Portfolio
Collect evidence of how you presented your findings and how you helped others learn. Include this in your portfolio as proof of the learning outcome: "letting others learn from you".
Portfolio evidence: slides, notes, session recording, feedback

Session Format Guidance

A half-day meeting is not the recommended format. Short, focused sessions work better.

Suggested structure: Each person gets ~5 minutes to present their key findings, followed by a group discussion. This gets things started without feeling like a formal presentation.

Groups can run multiple shorter sessions if needed — format should match the group's dynamic and be enjoyable for everyone involved.

Your Action Checklist

Write your 1-page pitch (use AI to help, include keywords)
Submit pitch to Canvas: "Pitch of your personal flavour"
Browse other pitches and vote on Teams form
Coordinate with your matched group and schedule a sharing session
Collect portfolio evidence from the session
Learning Outcome Connection

This activity directly maps to the learning outcome requiring you to "let others learn from you". Your portfolio evidence from Step 5 — how you presented, what you shared, and how you supported your peers' understanding — is the proof of this outcome.
